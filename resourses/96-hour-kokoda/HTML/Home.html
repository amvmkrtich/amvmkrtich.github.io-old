The Kokoda Trail

A superb trek into the heartland of Papua New Guinea, the Kokoda Trail holds a very important place in Australian history.

Commencing at the village of Kokoda, the trek follows the 96 kilometer Kokoda Trail across the Owen Stanley Range to Owerâ€™s Corner, through some of the most rugged and wild jungle in the world. Built over 200 years ago as a commuting route between villages, the track became the scene of bitter fighting between Australian and Japanese soldiers.

It was on Kokoda that the myth of Japanese invincibility was broken for the first time. Today battle sites are still evident throughout the jungle, marked by the weapon pits of the combatants.
